The War on Corporate Tyranny

In 2024, a shadowy elite of billionaires and corporations poured over $1.2 billion into Donald Trump’s campaign, hijacking democracy to serve their greed. These mega-donors—from tech titans to casino moguls—bankroll policies that torch environmental protections, widen wealth gaps, and crush workers’ rights. OpenSecrets reports a 25% spike in such donations since 2020, with Trump’s super PACs like MAGA Inc. and America PAC awash in their cash. Their agenda? Deregulation, tax cuts for the 1%, and unchecked corporate power.

We’re fighting back with the people’s weapon: boycotts. From the Montgomery Bus Boycott’s civil rights triumph to #GrabYourWallet’s $1B+ hit on Trump-aligned brands, consumer power reshapes history. In 2024, boycotts cut 4-7% of revenue for targeted donors like Tesla and Uline, per industry data. Join our global movement to starve their influence, support ethical brands, and demand a democracy where people, not plutocrats, rule.

Your Battle Plan: Boycott with Precision

Turn your wallet into a weapon with these seven proven steps, backed by decades of boycott victories:

  1. Target Their Brands: Avoid mega-donor companies like Tesla or Uline. Use apps like Buycott or GoodOnYou to find ethical alternatives, such as Rivian for EVs or EcoEnclose for packaging. Ethical Consumer rates brands on transparency and labor practices.
  2. Rally Your Network: Amplify your pledge with #BoycottTrumpDonors. A 2024 Pew study shows 70% of consumers trust peer recommendations. Share your story on X, tag allies, and join boycott forums to spark a movement.
  3. Track Your Impact: Log your boycott progress below. Cutting 80% of spending on a donor brand can save you $500/year while costing them millions collectively. Share milestones to inspire others.
  4. Organize Locally: Host rallies, flyer campaigns, or ethical business fairs. Our Event Toolkit includes templates and guides. In 2024, 500+ local events drove boycott adoption.
  5. Know Your Enemy: Study donor influence via OpenSecrets.org or Jane Mayer’s Dark Money. Understanding their lobbying (e.g., Koch Industries’ anti-climate push) fuels smarter boycotts.
  6. Advocate for Reform: Push for campaign finance laws to curb billionaire donations. Contact Congress via Public Citizen and vote for reform candidates. In 2024, 60% of voters backed donation caps, per Gallup.
  7. Stay Resilient: Mega-donors will fight back with PR spins. Counter their narratives on social media, support independent journalism, and keep boycotting. Persistence won the 1955 bus boycott— it’ll win here too.
