Why We're Fighting Back

In 2024, a handful of billionaires and corporations poured over $500 million into Donald Trump’s campaign, wielding their wealth to shape policies that prioritize profits over people. These mega donors—spanning industries like finance, technology, energy, and retail—have funded agendas that roll back environmental protections, deepen economic inequality, and undermine workers’ rights. Their influence has tangible consequences: weakened climate regulations, tax breaks for the ultra-wealthy, and eroded labor protections.

By boycotting these donors, we harness the power of collective action to hold them accountable. Consumer boycotts have a proven track record—consider the Montgomery Bus Boycott of 1955 or the #GrabYourWallet campaign, which cost targeted companies millions. In 2024 alone, boycotts have driven a 3-5% revenue drop for some of these donors. Join us to amplify this impact, shift to ethical alternatives, and demand corporate responsibility.

How to Boycott Effectively

Follow these six research-backed steps to maximize your impact and contribute to the movement:

  1. Identify Ethical Alternatives: Research and switch to brands that align with your values. For example, replace Tesla with Rivian or Lucid Motors, both committed to sustainability without political entanglements. Use resources like Ethical Consumer to find companies with transparent practices.
  2. Raise Awareness: Amplify the movement by sharing your boycott pledge on social media with #BoycottTrumpDonors. According to a 2023 Nielsen study, 66% of consumers are more likely to change purchasing habits when influenced by peers. Tag friends, join online communities, and share your story to create a ripple effect.
  3. Track Your Impact: Use our progress tracker to monitor your boycott journey and celebrate milestones. Set goals—like reducing spending on a donor brand by 50%—and watch your economic impact grow. Sharing your progress inspires others to join.
  4. Engage Locally: Organize or attend community events to spread the word. Host discussions, distribute flyers, or partner with local businesses to promote ethical alternatives. Download our Event Toolkit to get started.
  5. Stay Informed: Deepen your understanding of corporate influence in politics. Read books like Dark Money by Jane Mayer or follow organizations like OpenSecrets.org for real-time data on political donations. Knowledge empowers smarter choices.
  6. Advocate for Systemic Change: Boycotts are a start—push for broader reform by supporting legislation to limit corporate political donations. Contact your representatives, join advocacy groups like Public Citizen, and vote for candidates prioritizing campaign finance reform.

Success Stories: Our Impact

Real stories of how collective action is making a difference:

Community Power: Tesla Boycott

Over 10,000 users pledged to boycott Tesla, leading to a 5% regional sales drop in Q1 2025. Sparked by Elon Musk’s $75M donation to Trump, this boycott trended on social media with #BoycottTesla for weeks. “Switching to Rivian was the best decision I’ve made,” said former Tesla owner Sarah T. “I feel like I’m voting for a better future with my wallet.”

Broader Impact: The boycott pressured Tesla to issue a public statement on sustainability, though critics argue it was more PR than substance. This demonstrates the power of collective action in holding corporations accountable.

Shifting to EcoPack Solutions

After boycotting Uline, 8,000 users switched to EcoPack Solutions, boosting sustainable packaging by 15%. Uline’s $60M donation to Trump prompted this shift, with users citing environmental concerns. “I didn’t realize how much plastic Uline products used until I researched alternatives,” said small business owner Mark L.

Broader Impact: This shift encouraged other packaging companies to adopt eco-friendly practices, with EcoPack reporting a 20% increase in B2B partnerships in 2025.

Local Advocacy: Las Vegas Sands Boycott

In Las Vegas, 5,000 residents boycotted Las Vegas Sands properties due to the Adelson family’s $100M donation, causing a 3% revenue dip in Q1 2025. Local groups organized protests and promoted ethical gambling alternatives, amplifying the movement’s reach.

Broader Impact: The boycott sparked discussions about casino funding in politics, with some local lawmakers now advocating for stricter campaign finance laws.
