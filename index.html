EXPOSED: Trump's Top 10 Mega Donors

These billionaires are funding Trump's agenda—fight back by boycotting their brands!

Why We’re Fighting Back

In 2024, a select group of billionaires and corporations contributed over $500 million to Donald Trump’s campaign, wielding unprecedented influence over political decisions that impact millions. These mega donors, often tied to industries like finance, tech, and energy, are using their wealth to shape policies that prioritize profit over people—policies that have led to environmental deregulation, tax cuts for the ultra-wealthy, and weakened labor protections.

By boycotting these mega donors, we’re sending a clear message: our wallets can be a powerful tool for change. Historically, consumer boycotts have driven significant corporate accountability—think of the Montgomery Bus Boycott of 1955 or the more recent #GrabYourWallet movement. In 2024 alone, targeted boycotts have already cost complicit corporations millions in lost revenue. Together, we can amplify this impact, hold these donors accountable, and push for a more ethical economy.

How to Boycott Effectively

Follow these detailed steps to maximize the impact of your boycott and contribute to the movement:

  1. Research Alternatives: Identify ethical alternatives for each brand you’re boycotting. For example, if you’re boycotting Tesla, consider brands like Rivian or Lucid Motors, which prioritize sustainability. Use tools like Ethical Consumer to find companies aligned with your values.
  2. Spread Awareness: Share your pledge on social media using #BoycottTrumpDonors to inspire others. Create a ripple effect by tagging friends, joining online communities, and posting about your experience. According to a 2023 study by Nielsen, 66% of consumers are more likely to change their buying habits when influenced by peers.
  3. Track Your Progress: Use our progress tracker to monitor your boycott journey and celebrate milestones. Set personal goals—like reducing your spending with a specific brand by 50%—and track your impact over time. Share your achievements to motivate others!
  4. Engage Locally: Organize or join local events to amplify the movement. Host a community discussion, create flyers, or partner with local businesses to promote ethical alternatives. Check out volunteer opportunities below to get involved in grassroots efforts.
  5. Stay Informed: Deepen your understanding by reading articles, reports, and books on corporate influence in politics. Recommended reads include Dark Money by Jane Mayer and The Hidden History of Big Money in Politics by Thom Hartmann. Follow organizations like OpenSecrets.org for the latest data on political donations.
  6. Advocate for Systemic Change: Boycotting is just the start—push for broader change by supporting legislation that limits corporate political donations. Contact your representatives, join advocacy groups like Public Citizen, and vote for candidates who prioritize campaign finance reform.

Success Stories: Our Impact

Community Power: Tesla Boycott

Over 10,000 users pledged to boycott Tesla, leading to a 5% drop in regional sales in Q1 2025. This boycott, sparked by Elon Musk’s $75M donation to Trump, gained traction on social media with #BoycottTesla trending for weeks. "Switching to Rivian was the best decision I’ve made," says Sarah T., a former Tesla owner. "I feel like I’m voting with my dollars for a better future."

Broader Impact: The boycott also pressured Tesla to issue a public statement on sustainability, though critics argue it’s more PR than substance. This shows the power of collective action in holding corporations accountable.

Switching to EcoPack Solutions

After boycotting Uline, 8,000 users switched to EcoPack Solutions, boosting sustainable packaging by 15%. Uline’s $60M donation to Trump prompted this shift, with users citing environmental concerns. "I didn’t realize how much plastic Uline products used until I researched alternatives," says Mark L., a small business owner.

Broader Impact: This shift has encouraged other packaging companies to adopt eco-friendly practices, with EcoPack reporting a 20% increase in B2B partnerships in 2025.

Local Advocacy: Las Vegas Sands Boycott

In Las Vegas, 5,000 residents boycotted Las Vegas Sands properties after the Adelson Family’s $100M donation to Trump. This led to a 3% dip in Sands’ quarterly revenue. Local groups organized protests and promoted ethical gaming alternatives, amplifying the movement.

Broader Impact: The boycott sparked discussions about the role of casino money in politics, with some local lawmakers now advocating for stricter campaign finance laws.
