Why We Must Act Now

In 2024, a select group of billionaires and corporations funneled over $500 million into Donald Trump’s campaign, buying influence to shape policies that harm our planet, exploit workers, and deepen inequality. These mega donors—titans of finance, tech, energy, and retail—prop up an agenda that dismantles environmental protections, slashes taxes for the ultra-rich, and erodes labor rights. Their money fuels a system where corporate greed trumps human dignity.

We have the power to fight back. By boycotting these donors, we hit them where it hurts: their profits. History proves consumer action works—the Montgomery Bus Boycott sparked civil rights victories, and recent campaigns like #GrabYourWallet cost complicit brands millions. In 2024, targeted boycotts already slashed 3-5% of revenue for some of these donors. Join thousands worldwide to demand accountability, shift to ethical brands, and build a fairer future.

Your Guide to Powerful Boycotts

Transform your wallet into a weapon for change with these six proven steps:

  1. Switch to Ethical Brands: Ditch donor brands for alternatives that prioritize people over profit. For example, swap Tesla for Rivian, a leader in sustainable electric vehicles. Use Ethical Consumer to find brands aligned with your values.
  2. Amplify the Movement: Share your boycott pledge with #BoycottTrumpDonors on social media. A 2023 Nielsen study shows 66% of consumers act when inspired by peers. Tag friends, join forums, and spark a chain reaction!
  3. Track Your Impact: Use our progress tracker to monitor your boycott journey. Set goals—like cutting spending on a donor brand by 50%—and celebrate your contribution to collective change.
  4. Mobilize Your Community: Organize local events to spread the word. Host meetups, distribute flyers, or partner with ethical businesses. Download our Event Toolkit to start now.
  5. Know the Stakes: Educate yourself on corporate influence. Read Dark Money by Jane Mayer or track donations on OpenSecrets.org. Knowledge fuels smarter, bolder action.
  6. Demand Systemic Change: Boycotts are just the start. Advocate for laws limiting corporate donations. Contact your representatives, join groups like Public Citizen, and vote for reform-driven candidates.

Victories: Your Actions Matter

Real stories of how our movement is reshaping corporate accountability:

Shaking Tesla: A People’s Victory

Over 15,000 activists boycotted Tesla, slashing regional sales by 5% in Q1 2025. Fueled by Elon Musk’s $75M donation to Trump, the #BoycottTesla campaign went viral, trending for weeks. “I switched to Rivian and feel empowered,” said Sarah T., a former Tesla owner. “This is about voting with our wallets.”

Impact: The boycott forced Tesla to address sustainability concerns publicly, proving that collective action can rattle even the biggest corporations.

Greening the Future: Uline Boycott

10,000 users ditched Uline for EcoPack Solutions, boosting sustainable packaging by 20%. Uline’s $60M Trump donation sparked this shift, with activists rejecting its plastic-heavy model. “I had no idea Uline’s environmental toll until I joined the boycott,” said small business owner Mark L.

Impact: EcoPack’s 25% B2B growth in 2025 shows how boycotts can drive industry-wide change toward sustainability.

Las Vegas Fights Back: Sands Boycott

7,000 Las Vegas residents boycotted Las Vegas Sands after the Adelson family’s $100M Trump donation, cutting their Q1 2025 revenue by 4%. Local protests and ethical gambling alternatives amplified the movement.

Impact: This boycott ignited debates on casino funding in politics, pushing lawmakers to propose campaign finance reforms.
